Introduction

The West African Examinations Council has created e-channels for most of its services. These channels allow customers easy, fast, and reliable access to various Council services.

Some of the E-channels include:

  • WAEC E-Learning
  • WAEC Request Management System
  • WAEC Digital Certificate
  • WAEC Candidate's Notice of Malpractice Portal
  • WAEC Certificate Request Portal
  • WAEC Edustat
WAEC E-Learning

The primary aim of this initiative is to equip students for better performance in future examinations.

On this platform (waeconline.org.ng) you will find resources for all WAEC-approved subjects that will help you understand the standards required for success.

WAEC Request Management System

The online self-service portal (request.waec.ng) reduces the need for physical presence at WAEC offices.

Candidates purchase a customer support ticket online to access services and track their request status. There are two types of tokens:

  • Request for information
  • Request for service
WAEC Digital Certificate

The digital certificate platform (waec.org) enables candidates to share their original certificates instantly, from anywhere.

Steps:

  • Create an account
  • Log in
  • Follow on-screen instructions
Candidates Notice of Malpractice Portal

This portal (waecinternational.org/complaints) allows candidates whose results were withheld to view reasons for sanctions and provide relevant information.

WAEC Certificate Request Portal

Private candidates can now request certificates online. The certificates can be delivered to any WAEC office of choice, with valid ID required for collection.

Portal: certrequest.waec.ng

WAEC Edustat

The WAEC Edustat portal (waec.edustat.ng) gives stakeholders access to statistics on WAEC activities.

Users include:

  • Government
  • Researchers
  • Parents
  • School owners
  • Students
  • NGOs
